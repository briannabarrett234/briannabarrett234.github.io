Subject Areas

Technological Applied Studies

Food Technology:Provides knowledge and skills in food preparation, service, and management. Students learn about cooking techniques, menu planning, and the operation of hospitality businesses.

Timber:Involves the study and application of manufacturing and engineering technologies. Students work with materials like metal, wood, and plastics, and learn about processes such as machining and assembly.

Computing:Covers the use of computers and digital systems to solve problems and manage information. Topics include software development, networking, and digital media.

Textiles:Involves the study of textile materials, design, and production processes. Students create their own textile products, such as clothing or accessories, and learn about the fashion and textile industries.

Design and Technology:Encourages students to design, develop, and evaluate technological solutions to real-world problems. Students work on projects involving product design, engineering principles, and material selection.

Engineering:Provides a foundation in various engineering disciplines, including mechanical, electrical, and civil engineering. Students work on projects that involve designing and testing engineering solutions.
